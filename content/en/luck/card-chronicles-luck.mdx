### Understanding the RNG Roll System

Rolling is the absolute engine of progression in Card Chronicles. Your base luck starts at 1x, but it can be scaled exponentially. The higher your multiplier, the greater your chances of bypassing lower-tier common cards and landing S-Tier mythical or chronicle units.

Here is a breakdown of how different luck sources combine to form your total roll multiplier:

| Luck Source | Type | Multiplier / Bonus | How to Unlock |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Roblox Group** | Passive | +2 Luck | Join the Chronicle Entertainment group |
| **Friend Bonus** | Passive | +0.15x per Friend | Play in a lobby with active friends |
| **Milestones** | Passive | Up to +1.5x | Complete difficult in-game achievements |
| **Constellations** | Passive | Up to +12% Luck | Invest points into the skill tree |
| **Luck Potion III** | Active | +15% Luck | Craft or redeem via active codes |
| **Super Luck Potion** | Active | High Multiplier | Purchase from shop or get via codes |
| **Moon Cycles** | Event | 1.75x to 3.5x Luck | Wait for specific map weather phases |

### Moon Cycles and Weather Events

The map in Card Chronicles periodically shifts through different moon cycles and weather phases, such as **Graveyard** and **Inferno**. These cycles introduce massive global luck multipliers that apply to all active players.

| Weather Phase | Luck Multiplier | Special Effects | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal Phase** | 1.0x (Base) | Standard roll rates | Save high-value potions |
| **Inferno Phase** | 1.75x Luck | Increased fire-type card rates | Use standard Luck Potions |
| **Graveyard Phase** | 3.5x Luck | Unlocks rare dark-type cards | **Pop Super Luck & Roll Speed Potions** |

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Waste Your Boost Windows">
Global weather phases like the 3.5x Graveyard Phase are time-limited. Never activate your potions unless a high-multiplier moon cycle is active, and ensure you have enough inventory space to roll continuously without interruption.

Duplicate cards are inevitable in an RNG game, but they should not go to waste.
