Roblox Card Chronicles combines the thrill of anime card RNG with strategic card battling. Players roll for unique cards, upgrade their abilities, assemble powerful lineups, and challenge endless waves of enemies. Succeeding in this game requires a clear understanding of how to manage your resources and when to invest in your collection. To build a solid foundation, you must understand the relationship between rolling volume, card rarity, and combat upgrades. While high-rarity cards are the ultimate goal, an upgraded lower-rarity card will often outperform a fresh, unupgraded rare pull.

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Progression Route">

Navigating the early stages of Card Chronicles requires a structured approach. Without a plan, it is easy to run out of gold, potions, or rerolls before you have built a team capable of pushing past wave-based milestones. Your immediate goal is to establish a self-sustaining loop: roll for cards, build a functional lineup, clear waves to secure resources, and reinvest those resources into your strongest units.

Following a disciplined progression route ensures that you do not hit early walls that require tedious grinding to overcome.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Roll for a Usable Starter Pool">
    Begin by rolling to expand your basic card collection. Do not worry about securing ultra-rare cards immediately. Your goal in this step is to find three to four cards with decent base stats and usable combat abilities so you can form your very first active lineup.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble a Focused Lineup">
    Place your strongest available cards into your active team slots. Focus on units that offer a balance of wave-clearing damage and survivability. Avoid putting unupgraded cards into your lineup just because they look appealing; utility is far more important than appearance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Concentrate Upgrades on Your Carry">
    Select your single best damage-dealing card and focus your upgrade materials on it. A single highly-upgraded carry card can easily clear early waves, whereas a team of multiple low-level units will struggle to defeat tougher enemies and bosses.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy Potions Strategically">
    Activate Luck Potions, Super Luck Potions, and Roll Speed Potions only when you are ready for a dedicated rolling session. Do not waste these valuable consumables if you plan to go idle or if your inventory is full of unmanaged cards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Apply Trait Rerolls to Keeper Cards">
    Once you acquire a high-tier card that you plan to keep in your lineup for the long term, use your accumulated Trait Rerolls to optimize its stats. This ensures you do not waste rare reroll resources on units that will soon be replaced.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Push Endless Waves and Identify Weaknesses">
    Challenge the wave stages to earn rewards and test your team's limits. When you eventually fail a wave, identify the weakest card in your lineup and focus on upgrading or replacing that specific slot before attempting the wave again.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Stage | Primary Goal | Recommended Lineup Setup | Resource Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early Game** | Establish a basic card pool and reach wave 20. | One upgraded carry, two basic supports. | Gold and basic upgrade materials. |
| **Mid Game** | Unlock advanced abilities and reach 25k rolls. | Two highly-upgraded carries, one utility unit. | Luck Potions and code reward redemption. |
| **Late Game** | Optimize traits and push high endless waves. | Fully upgraded S-Tier carries with rolled traits. | Trait Rerolls and Super Luck Potions. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Progression Warning">
Never activate multiple progression potions if you are distracted or planning to log off soon. Potions run in real-time, and letting them expire while idle is a massive waste of progression speed.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trait Rerolls & Upgrade Strategy">

Traits act as powerful modifiers that can drastically alter a card's combat performance. Securing a strong trait on an S-Tier carry can double its damage output or utility. However, Trait Rerolls are a limited resource, making strategic conservation incredibly important for long-term progression.

You can secure a large number of Trait Rerolls early on by redeeming active promotional codes. For example, codes like `AvailableForEveryoneNow` provide 100 Trait Rerolls, while codes like `Tyfor3kccu!` and `Sorry!` offer generous bundles of potions and rerolls.

| Resource | Primary Acquisition | Best Target for Use | Common Mistake to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gold** | Clearing waves and completing daily challenges. | Upgrading your primary and secondary carries. | Spreading gold evenly across every card. |
| **Trait Rerolls** | Code redemptions and high-tier wave rewards. | Fully upgraded S-Tier cards with proven utility. | Rerolling traits on temporary starter units. |
| **Luck Potions** | Promo codes and progression milestones. | Dedicated, high-speed rolling sessions. | Activating potions during casual play. |
| **Chronogems** | Special reward bundles and elite achievements. | High-value progression unlocks and rare items. | Spending gems on low-priority items. |

When managing your upgrades, always follow a top-heavy investment model. Put 70% of your available gold into your primary wave-clear unit, 20% into your secondary support or utility card, and keep the remaining 10% in reserve for inventory management or emergency upgrades.

<Callout type="info" title="Code Strategy">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Lineup Logic and Team Building">

Building a successful lineup in Card Chronicles is about creating synergy, not just stacking your rarest cards. A balanced team must be able to handle both large groups of weak enemies and single, high-health bosses. If your team consists entirely of single-target damage dealers, you will quickly be overwhelmed by swarm waves. Conversely, a team with only area-of-effect damage may struggle to defeat major bosses.

To optimize your team, consider these four distinct lineup builds tailored for different stages and challenges of the game.

| Lineup Type | Core Concept | Primary Carry Focus | Support / Utility Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early Wave Clear** | Fast, efficient clearing of early-stage swarms. | High area-of-effect damage. | Basic stat boosters or fast-attack units. |
| **Boss Push** | High single-target damage to defeat elite foes. | Boss-killer cards with high single-hit damage. | Defensive or crowd-control utility cards. |
| **Reroll-Focused** | Built specifically to maximize a single rolled trait. | A card with a powerful, specialized trait. | Units that complement the carry's trait. |
| **Balanced Progress** | A versatile setup designed to handle general content. | One strong carry with balanced damage. | One area-of-effect unit, one single-target unit. |

When building your team, always evaluate how your cards interact. If your main carry relies on critical strikes, look for support cards or traits that increase critical strike chance or lower enemy defenses. This cooperative synergy is what allows advanced players to clear waves that are technically above their team's raw power rating.

<Callout type="tip" title="Lineup Tip">
Regularly review your combat logs during wave attempts. If you notice a specific card is consistently dealing less than 10% of your team's total damage, it is time to replace it with a utility unit or a more efficient damage dealer.
</Callout>

</Section>
