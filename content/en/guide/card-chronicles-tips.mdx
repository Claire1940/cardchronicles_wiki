Rolling is your primary method of acquiring new units. When you first start, your goal should be to build a solid pool of characters rather than obsessively chasing a single high-rarity unit. Spreading your resources too thin is a common mistake; instead, identify one or two strong carries and focus your gold and materials on upgrading them. Additionally, keep an eye on your inventory space, as a full inventory will halt your rolling momentum.

<Callout type="info" title="Inventory Management">
Merging duplicate cards immediately is highly recommended. Not only does this free up valuable space in your inventory, but it also elevates your cards to stronger tiers like Gold, Magmatic, and Abyssal, significantly boosting their combat stats.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Lineup Strategy: Building the Best Team">

Your lineup is the heart of your combat efficiency. In Card Chronicles, you have the unique ability to utilize multiple combat slots simultaneously, allowing you to fight more than one battle at once! This makes lineup synergy and role distribution incredibly important. A balanced team should feature a high-damage main carry, supported by units that provide utility, crowd control, or stat buffs.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Main Carry Cards">
    - **High-rarity units** with devastating active damage abilities.
    - Worth investing your gold and **Trait Rerolls** into.
    - Focus upgrades here first to clear waves quickly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Support Utility Cards">
    - Units that provide **dodge chance**, healing, or shield buffs.
    - Even lower-rarity support cards can become overpowered at higher tiers.
    - Keep them leveled to sustain your main carries.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Crowd Control Cards">
    - Units that feature **on-entry stun** or freeze mechanics.
    - Crucial for delaying powerful boss attacks.
    - Essential for pushing high-difficulty tower floors.
  </Card>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Spreading Resources">
Do not upgrade every new card you pull. Spreading your resources across too many low-rarity or temporary placeholder cards will leave you weak when facing major bosses. Pick a core team and stick with them.
</Callout>

When selecting your team, keep in mind that card rarity directly dictates stat scaling and support efficiency. For example, a base support card might offer a 1% dodge chance, but that same card scaled to Magmatic or Abyssal can offer up to 7.5% dodge or higher. Always prioritize upgrading cards that scale well into the late-game.
