<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Trait Rerolls and Power Optimization">

Traits are the hidden modifiers that can turn a good card into a god-tier unit. However, Trait Rerolls are a finite resource (outside of generous code drops). Learning when to "hold" and when to "reroll" is a skill that separates veteran players from beginners.

**Trait Priority Logic:**
1. **Identify the Keeper**: Only reroll traits on cards you intend to use for at least 50+ waves.
2. **Check the Ability**: Some traits synergize better with specific card abilities (e.g., speed traits on slow, heavy hitters).
3. **Save for Events**: Occasionally, events might offer better trait outcomes or specialized modifiers.

<Callout type="info" title="Trait Management">
The code "AvailableForEveryoneNow" provides 100 Trait Rerolls. This is an enormous boost for new players. Use these on your first A-Tier or S-Tier pull to secure a strong early-game lead.
</Callout>

| Trait Type | General Effect | Ideal For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack Power** | Increases raw damage output | All Carry units |
| **Attack Speed** | Decreases time between strikes | Units with on-hit effects |
| **Critical Chance** | Higher chance for massive damage | High-rarity DPS units |
| **Luck Boost** | Small boost to secondary roll luck | Support or utility slots |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Lineup Builds for Waves and Bosses">

Building a lineup in Card Chronicles is about more than just picking your rarest cards. You need to consider the "Wave Wall"—the point where enemies become too tanky for basic damage. A successful lineup balances immediate wave clear with sustained boss pressure.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Wave Clear Core">
    Start with your most upgraded unit. This unit's job is to delete the small mobs quickly so they don't overwhelm your defense.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Boss Specialist">
    Include at least one unit with high single-target damage. Bosses appear periodically and require focused fire to take down within the time limit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Flex Slot">
    The third slot should be used for testing new pulls or adding a utility unit that provides buffs or debuffs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Build Name | Focus | Core Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early Wave Clear** | Speed | Use high-speed A-tier units to farm low waves |
| **Boss Push** | Single Target | Focus all upgrades on one high-damage S-tier unit |
| **Balanced Progress** | Stability | One carry, one sub-DPS, one utility/test unit |
| **Reroll Focused** | Long-term | Build around a unit with a Perfect/Mythic trait |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Lineup Tip">
If you are stuck on a wave, try swapping your "Flex Slot" for a unit with a different damage type or ability. Sometimes a lower-rarity card with a better ability match-up can break the wall.
</Callout>

</Section>

Potions are your primary tools for accelerating the card chronicles guide experience. Because they are timed, you should never activate them unless you are prepared to play for the full duration. Stacking potions is often more efficient than using them one by one.

**Common Potion Types:**
- **Luck Potion**: Increases the chance of higher rarity rolls.
- **Roll Speed Potion**: Decreases the interval between automatic rolls.
- **Battle Speed Potion**: Speeds up the combat animations for faster wave clearing.
- **Boss Potion**: Specifically aids in progression against strong foes.
