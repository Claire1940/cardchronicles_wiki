<Section icon={Swords} title="Card Chronicles How to Play: Rolling and Rarity Mechanics">

Understanding the rolling and rarity systems is essential when learning **card chronicles how to play**. Rolling is the heart of the game, and your success depends on how efficiently you manage your luck multipliers and recognize which cards are worth keeping.

The game features several rarity tiers that dictate a card's base power and scaling potential. Higher rarity cards naturally possess stronger base attributes, but an upgraded mid-tier card can often outperform a fresh, unupgraded high-rarity pull.

| Rarity Tier | Base Power Level | Ability Scaling | Upgrade Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chronicle** | Extremely High | Exceptional | **Highest** (Always upgrade first) |
| **Mystic** | Very High | Strong | **High** (Excellent long-term carry) |
| **Abyssal** | High | Good | **Medium-High** (Reliable mid-game option) |
| **Magmatic** | Moderate | Average | **Medium** (Decent placeholder) |
| **Gold** | Low-Moderate | Low | **Low** (Replace as soon as possible) |
| **Base** | Low | Minimal | **Lowest** (Use only as fusion fodder) |

To maximize your rolling efficiency, always stack your luck boosts. Using a Luck Potion alongside a Super Luck Potion increases your chances of pulling Mystic and Chronicle cards significantly. Additionally, increasing your index level by discovering new cards provides permanent passive luck and roll speed bonuses.

<Callout type="warning" title="Rarity Trap">
Do not assume that a newly pulled rare card is automatically better than your heavily upgraded lower-rarity cards. Always compare their actual battle performance and wave-clearing capabilities before changing your lineup.
</Callout>
