<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Beginner Progression">

Navigating the early hours of the **card chronicles roblox release** requires a disciplined approach to resource allocation. New players often make the mistake of spreading their upgrades too thin across every card they roll. Instead, follow this structured path to ensure your lineup remains viable for higher waves.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Rolling Phase">
    Start by rolling for a basic pool of cards. Do not worry about rarity immediately; your goal is to fill your active slots so you can begin combat. Use your first few rolls to identify cards with decent base damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Lineup Formation">
    Place your strongest available cards into your lineup. In the early game, focus on cards that have high attack speed or multi-target abilities, as these are most effective at clearing the initial waves of enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Strategic Upgrading">
    Select one or two "carry" cards that consistently perform well in fights. Invest your resources into upgrading these specific units to unlock their advanced abilities. An upgraded A-tier card is often better than a base S-tier card with no investment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Potion Activation">
    Use your **Luck Potions** and **Roll Speed Potions** during dedicated rolling sessions. Do not activate these boosts if you only plan to play for a few minutes. Maximize the timer by ensuring your inventory is clear and you are ready to roll continuously.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Trait Management">
    Once you have a "keeper" card that you plan to use for the next 50+ waves, apply your **Trait Rerolls**. Look for traits that complement the card's role, such as damage boosts for attackers or survivability for frontline units.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Progression Strategy">
If you hit a wall in the endless waves, return to the rolling screen. Use the Chronogems earned from your combat sessions to roll for better rarities, then swap out your weakest lineup slot for the new pull.
</Callout>
